Previous Projects

Ecocycle

The idea behind Ecocycle is designed to address the growing concern of electronic waste (e-waste) management in the country. In this era where people rely on their electronic devices to do even the most simple task, the accumulation of electronic waste has become a pressing issue worldwide, as improper disposal of these devices poses a significant threat to the environment.


This app prototype aims to empower users to dispose of their electronics properly. By giving them a platform where they could easily find places and projects that accept used electronic parts, and also allowing them to connect with like-minded people and contribute to their projects, the app fosters a culture of environmental stewardship, and encourages sustainable practices. We leverage the power of technology to address the challenges surrounding the proper use and disposal of electronic devices, for a cleaner, greener, future.


Early Wireframes


Inspiration

The main challenge lies in the lack of awareness, accessibility, and accountability surrounding e-waste disposal. Despite the continuous growth in the usage of electronic devices, proper disposal practices are still not being observed, as will be presented in the upcoming sections. This could result in environmental damage, as e-waste usually ends up in landfills or is improperly handled, resulting in pollution and resource depletion.


Our application aims to address these challenges by creating a user-centric e-waste management app that streamlines the disposal process of e-waste, promotes recycling of used electronic components, and empowering users to care for the environment. Specifically, it is done by educating users about responsible e-waste disposal practices, simplifying the process of finding certified e-waste drop off locations, and connecting users with responsible recyclers


Brainstorm Sessions


Prototype

For this application, we were able to come up with three designs, as we felt like while the first two designs give off care for the environment vibes, it does not really catch the technological aspect of the purpose of our app.




Feedback

With the use of Google forms as our medium of feedback reception, we categorized our feedback into functionality and design.


Most respondents found that the application was seen favorably in terms of both functionality and design. The scope of the application’s functionality include the navigation capabilities of the application, the expected output of the functionalities, as well as the clarity of usage of the value propositions the application can provide.



Final Concept

The final concept is a slight modification of the 3rd redesign of our application prototype. Dark cool colors moving in a gradient with a minimalistic style and soft rounded elements is the UI design that we have finalized and worked with. The slight modifications are all related to the appearance of the application, including text enlargements, proper fixed navigation, icons made to be more clear and direct about where they would lead to, dropdowns not overlapping each other, and displaying the distance from a user to a drop-off site, so the UI Design was not be affected overall.


The EcoCycle prototype is the tool in which the majority of the regular people, electronic shop owners, and project makers can find and donate the leftover electronic components or devices that they may have. This method not only recycles broken electronics, but also raises awareness on e-waste disposal, as well as reducing potentially hazardous e-waste in the environment.
